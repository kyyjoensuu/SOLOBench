### Question 2: Word Origins and Geometry
**Question:** How do the words listed with their origin in the second part of the extract, such as "conic," relate to the concept of geometry and how it is applied in mathematics?

**Answer:**
The word "conic" is derived from the combination of "conope," meaning cone, and "-ia," which indicates a state. Conics are curves described by a second-degree polynomial equation. In mathematics, conics have great significance due to their extensive applications in various fields. Here are some key areas where they play a crucial role:

1. **Ellipses, Hyperbolas, and Parabolas**: These are different types of conic sections obtained by intersecting a cone with a plane. The equations for these shapes are:
   - Ellipse:
     \[
     \frac{x^2}{a^2} + \frac{y^2}{b^2} = 1
     \]
     Here, \(a\) and \(b\) are the semi-major and semi-minor axes of the ellipse.
   - Hyperbola:
     \[
     \frac{(x - h)^2}{a^2} - \frac{(y - k)^2}{b^2} = 1
     \]
     Here, \(h\) and \(k\) are the coordinates of the center, and \(a\) and \(b\) have different roles in defining the branches.
   - Parabola:
     \[
     y = ax^2 + bx + c
     \]
     Here, the curve is a plane parabola that opens upwards or downwards depending on the sign of \(a\).

The study of conic sections has deep historical roots and continues to be relevant in various fields such as physics, engineering, and mathematics.
