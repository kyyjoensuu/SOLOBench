1. Use British English (spelling, capitalization, punctuation) when appropriate
2. No repeated words. No repeated verbs, adverbs, adjectives or nouns.
3. Do not include any numbers in any sentences
4. Adjectives can be comparative or superlative where appropriate
5. Adjectives can end in 'ing' or 'ed' where appropriate
6. Adjectives should be appropriate to describe the nouns that follow in each sentence
7. Do not use words to describe other words within a sentence
8. Do not change the tense of the verb if you change the tense of the adjective e.g. use 'was' not 'were'.
9. When using possessive nouns (e.g. 's') count 's' as part of the noun and not as a separate word.
