### 2. **Physics Problems**
- **Newton's Laws of Motion**: If an object of mass \(m\) is acted upon by a net force \(F\), then the acceleration \(a\) is given by:
  \[ F = ma \]
  **Example**: If a force of 10 N acts on a 2 kg mass, the acceleration is:
  \[ a = \frac{F}{m} = \frac{10 \, \text{N}}{2 \, \text{kg}} = 5 \, \text{m/s}^2 \]

- **Centripetal Acceleration**: 
  \[ a_c = \omega^2 r \]
  **Example**: If an object is rotating at \( \omega = 4 \, \text{rad/s} \) with a radius \(r = 2 \, \text{m}\), the centripetal acceleration is:
  \[ a_c = (4 \, \text{rad/s})^2 \times 2 \, \text{m} = 32 \, \text{m/s}^2 \]

### 3. **Chemistry Problems**

- **Mean and Standard Deviation**:
  \[ \text{Mean}(\mu) = \frac{\sum x_i}{n} \]
  \[ \text{Standard Deviation}(\sigma) = \sqrt{\frac{\sum (x_i - \mu)^2}{n}} \]
  **Example**: For data points 1, 3, 5, 7, 9:
  \[ \text{Mean} = \frac{1+3+5+7+9}{5} = 5 \]
  \[ \text{Standard Deviation} = \sqrt{\frac{(1-5)^2 + (3-5)^2 + (5-5)^2 + (7-5)^2 + (9-5)^2}{5}} = \sqrt{\frac{40}{5}} = \sqrt{8} = 2.83 \]
